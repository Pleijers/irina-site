Approach

I care more about how a dive feels than what the dive watch says.

I started freediving in Lanzarote in 2020, and became an instructor in 2023. Somewhere along the way I noticed that the divers who chase numbers get stuck, and the ones who work on everything else just quietly get deeper. So that is what we work on: equalization, relaxation, self-confidence, trust. Depth is not the goal. Depth is what happens when the rest is taken care of.

I am also a yoga practitioner, and it shows up in my teaching more often than I plan it to: the breath, the patience, the idea that you cannot force a body that does not feel safe.

And when I am not underwater with a student, I am usually underwater with a camera. Photography is my way of playing: no goals, no numbers, just looking at the sea until it gives you something.

Courses

Learn to freedive: AIDA 2 and AIDA 3, in Dahab.

Small, personal courses where technique and calm get equal attention. From your first real breath-hold (AIDA 2) to deeper, more confident diving (AIDA 3). All levels welcome; the sea does not care about your numbers, and neither do I.

Coaching

Personal coaching, in Dahab or online.

One-on-one coaching built around you: your breathing, your relaxation, your head. In the water in Dahab, or online if you are training elsewhere. We find what is actually holding you back, and it is almost never what you think it is.

EQ

Equalization, solved.

Equalization is the number one thing that stops freedivers, and the most fixable. As a SHARE EQ instructor I help you get from Valsalva to Frenzel, and from Frenzel to Mouthfill (evolved Frenzel), step by step. Start with a free call: we look at where you are and what would help.
